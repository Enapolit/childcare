Residential PV System Costs Rise as Raw Material Costs Surge

By Elizabeth Napolitano

April 05, 2022

Sunlight shines on solar panels laying on the ground (Photo Credit: Pixabay)

America’s solar energy industry has survived pandemic-driven global supply chain issues and shipping disruptions.But there’s one obstacle the solar industry might struggle to overcome — mounting prices.

Prices for residential photovoltaic (PV) systems have risen nearly 50% over the past year as the prices of component materials like polysilicon, steel, glass and copper continue to rise.

The situation could be a knife-edge for the thriving residential solar industry that has emerged along America’s sunbelt. That is because the long-term cost benefit of solar energy for consumers is what entices many homeowners to make the switch to solar.

But now, with prices rising and the end of government subsidies for home PV systems on the horizon, consumer interest in solar energy could wane.

Here are the materials that are causing PV system prices to hit new highs:

Frame

Steel is used for the frame of PV systems. Steel is up 215% since March 2020. In fact, hot-rolled steel climbed to a high of $1,825 this year. Before the pandemic, steel traded between $500 and $800.

Solar Cells

Polysilicon is used for the solar cells of PV systems. Polysilicon is up 300% — a ten-year high. The cost of solar panel raw material polysilicon topped $40/kg this month, according to the China Nonferrous Metals Association,

Junction Box

Copper is used in the junction boxes of PV systems. The price of copper increased 13-% over the past year. Copper prices soared to new highs in 2021 and broke records in March of 2022 when the cost of copper topped $5.00 per pound.
